Classification of Plants

The basic unit of classification is species, a group able to breed amongst themselves and bearing mutual resemblance, a broader classification is the genus. Several genera make up a family, and several families an order.

Highest level of classification/grouping is the 'Division'. There are about 14 Divisions

Some of the most common broad divisions are:

  • Angiosperms/ Magnoliopsida or Flowering plants. Major distinguishing characteristics: Flowers and fruit, vascular system with vessels. Approximate number of species described: 300,000
  • Coniferophyta or Conifers. Name Meaning: Pinus-like plant, cone-bearing plant. English Common Name: Conifers. Major distinguishing characteristics: Cones containing seeds and wood composed of tracheids. Approximate number of species described: 629 living
  • Hepatophyta or Liverworts. Name Meaning: Marchantia-like plant, liver plant. Major distinguishing characteristics: Ephemeral unbranched sporophytes, no vascular system. Approximate number of species described: 9,000
  • Cycadophyta or Cycads. Name Meaning: Cycas-like plant, palm-like plant. Major distinguishing characteristics: Seeds, crown of compound leaves. Approximate number of species described: 100 - 200
  • Bryophyta or Mosses. Name Meaning: Bryum-like plant, moss plant. Major distinguishing characteristics: Persistent unbranched sporophytes, no vascular system. Approximate number of species described: 12,000
